Our Strategy

What Have We Done?

Line Follower

We have 2 pairs of sensors with the sensors of the first pair placed close and 8cm in front of the main chassis, for accurate and fast line following, the second pair is to detect the green dots and sharper angles as the line follower would likely fail at angles less than 30. The sensors in this pair are placed near the chassis and about 6cm apart from each other.

Angular Rate Sensor

Instead of directly driving our motors at an angle we are using a Proportional gyro follower in which we are setting our target value to the output that we get from our line follower, this allows our bot to take accurate turns and correct itself from any error that might have been caused by slip, stall, speed breakers etc, This makes our bot one of the most stable ever

Mathematical Design

Derivative is a calculus function that finds the slope of a graph, in our case we are seeing how this slope changes and are using it to predict what is going to happen next. The algorithm for derivative is error minus last error (on a graph this would essentially draw a secant /tangent and then find the slope). This type of program can not function on its own, it needs a Proportional algorithm to function.

RGB Sensor Values

Each colour can be represented as a combination of a certain amount of Red, Green and Blue. Our colour sensors, instead of directly sensing the colour for the green dot, take the RGB value of the dot and if the value is similar to that of the calibrated green it does the required task.

Chassis

The chassis is essentially a tank. A tank offers a low centre of gravity and prevents slip, in turn, allowing the robot to navigate through rough terrain with ease and speed. This also allows the robot to effortlessly climb the ramps and overcome speed breakers with ease.

Rescue Zone Mechanism

We are using a forklift to hold, pick and drop the ‘Victims’ over the wall. We are using a gear mechanism to power the rack gears in the picking mechanism. The rack gears lift the victim above the wall so they can easily go over the wall and can easily be placed in the evacuation zone.
